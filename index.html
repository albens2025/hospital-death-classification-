Classification of Deaths in a Hospital Setting

This reference table provides a structured classification of hospital deaths based on timing, clinical context, and medico-legal implications. It serves as an educational and quality-improvement tool for junior staff, administrators, and medical professionals.

Code Circumstance & Timing Typical Mechanism(s) Key Medico-Legal Issues Usual Actions
A Dead on Arrival (DOA) Massive trauma, sudden cardiac death, electrocution, drowning Death at scene vs. hospital; coroner notification Certify brought-in-dead, inform police/coroner
B1 Peri-arrival death (<1 hour) Massive MI, tension pneumothorax, trauma Timeliness of care, ED activation Emergency audit; possible coroner case
B2 Early in-hospital death (1–24 hrs) Fulminant sepsis, intracranial bleed Diagnostic delay, failure to rescue M&M review
C Brief Acute Illness / Acute-on-Chronic Acute CHF, COPD exacerbation, DKA Escalation appropriateness Clinical audit
D Expected / Terminal Illness End-stage cancer, DNR in place Documentation of goals-of-care No reporting needed
E1 Death after high-risk procedure Transplant, high-risk cardiac surgery Informed consent, surgical risk Surgical M&M review
E2 Death after low-risk procedure Colonoscopy → perforation, elective surgery mishap Preventability, disclosure Sentinel event analysis
F Self-harm / Suicide-related Overdose, hanging, jump from height Risk assessment, psychiatric care gaps Mandatory coroner reporting
G1 Accidental in-hospital injury Falls, equipment-related trauma Environmental safety checks Incident reporting, RCA
G2 Therapy-induced harm Medication error, transfusion reaction System gaps, compliance failure Regulatory notification
G3 Hospital-acquired infection CLABSI, VAP, C. difficile colitis Infection control compliance Infection committee review
H Environmental / Social within hospital Fire, assault, power failure Security, building code violation Police + safety audit
